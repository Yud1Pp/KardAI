what is Cardiovascular?

Cardiovascular diseases are a group of disorders of the heart and blood vessels that include coronary heart disease, peripheral artery disease, and other related conditions. These diseases can lead to serious complications such as heart attack and stroke. It is important to maintain a healthy heart through a healthy diet, exercise, and regular check-ups.

Types of Cardivascular Disease

Arrhythmia

Arrhythmia is a heart rhythm disorder that occurs when the electrical signals in the heart do not function properly, causing the heart to beat too fast, slow, or irregularly.

Heart Failure

Heart failure is a condition where the heart cannot pump blood effectively enough to meet the body's needs, which can be caused by various factors such as coronary heart disease or hypertension.

Coronary Heart

Coronary heart disease occurs when the blood vessels that supply blood to the heart (coronary arteries) become blocked or narrowed due to fatty plaque buildup, increasing the risk of a heart attack.

Congenital Heart

Congenital heart defects refer to heart defects present from birth, involving disturbances in the structure or function of the heart that can affect blood flow.

Valve Disease

Heart valve disease involves abnormalities in the heart valves, which can cause the valves to not close properly or not open fully, affecting blood flow in the heart.

Blood
Vessel Blockage

Blood vessel blockage occurs when a blood vessel, either an artery or a vein, is blocked by plaque or a blood clot, which obstructs normal blood flow and could risk causing a stroke or heart attack.
